Child and Youth Protection Policy

Motion operates its services with the highest priority on the safety of children and youth. The following policy applies to all services provided by Motion.

1. Objectives of the Child and Youth Protection Policy

The company aims to protect children and youth from physically and mentally harmful environments and to create a safe and healthy service usage environment. To achieve this, we comply with the Youth Protection Act, the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection, and Google Play¡¯s Child Safety Standard Policy.

2. Access Restrictions and Management Measures for Children and Youth

To provide appropriate services for children and youth, the company implements the following management measures:

  1. Service Usage Age
    • All services provided by Motion are available only to individuals aged 13 and above. The terms and policies explicitly state that users must be at least 13 years old.
  2. Identity and Age Verification
    • To ensure that only real users access the service, a mobile phone identity verification process is strictly implemented during the sign-up process.
  3. Content Moderation and Monitoring
    • To maintain a healthy and safe interaction within the app, an automated moderation system scans and immediately removes sexually explicit images or videos upon upload. Continuous monitoring is conducted to detect and prevent harmful content and activities.
  4. Reporting and Enforcement
    • Any harmful information related to youth is promptly blocked and restricted through a reporting system. Additionally, users can report child safety concerns directly within the app.
  5. CSAE and CSAM Prevention
    • An in-app automatic filtering and monitoring system is in place to detect and remove CSAE (Child Sexual Abuse and Exploitation) and CSAM (Child Sexual Abuse Material).
  6. Compliance with Regulations and Reporting
    • The app complies with all relevant child safety laws. Any violations are immediately reported to local and national authorities.

3. Counseling and Grievance Handling for Harmful Information Affecting Youth

To minimize potential harm caused by harmful information, the company provides the following support measures:

  • If a youth experiences harm, they can be connected to the Teenage Women's Human Rights Center and the CYBER1388 Youth Counseling Center for counseling and assistance.
  • The company's customer support center accepts reports and complaints related to harmful content and provides assistance in resolving issues.

4. Education and Training for Child and Youth Protection Staff

The company provides training for child protection officers and service managers on the following topics:

  • Laws and regulations related to child and youth protection
  • Standards for enforcement actions
  • Methods for identifying harmful information
  • Procedures for handling violations and reporting
